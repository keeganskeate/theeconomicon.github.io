Attorny General Jeff Sessions Suggests Cannabis Use Leads to Amphetamine and Opioid Addiction

Thursday, October 31st 2017
By Robert Sagar

Dennis Yip/Flickr

Open ziplock bag spilling fentanyl pills across a table in a safe handling facility.

When asked to explain about his plans for the opioid crisis, Attorney General Sessions made the remark:

I do think that this whole country needs to be not so lackadaisical about drugs. And I even, if you talk to police chiefs, consistently they say much of the addiction starts with marijuana. It's not a harmless drug.

Attorney General Sessions continued to say that, "but, fundamentally, the big dangers, the big killers are the heroin, the fentanyl and carfentanil, and those kinds of things. Cocaine, methamphetamine are all coming in record amounts. You know it's compact, you can smuggle it in because you can get a lot of dosage units out of meth, a lot of dosage units out of heroin, lots of them, ten times as many, a hundred times as many out of fentanyl, it's the same impact. So, the challenge to law enforcement is really tough and we're going to be working on that. The president has given us the directive to work on it. That we will do."
